2.3 Seizures

October 31st, 2016

Definition

“Do not design content in a way that is known to cause seizures” - World Wide Web Consortium.

What's that mean?

The definition is fairly straightforward, but it is important to not design or develop anything on your website that flashes at a high rate as it could induce seizures.

Why?

It’s important to design content in a way that limits the number of flashes per second to ensure every user can access content in the same way. Content that may induce seizures could prevent some users from interacting with any of your content.

Example

A common culprit of this criterion are ads. Ads are traditionally designed and developed in a way that grab a user's attention. Many ads flash to draw your eyes to the content and hopefully get you to click on the ad. The issue is, if not considered, it can flash to a point that might induce seizures. The limit is 3 flashes per second. Any content that flashes more than 3 times in a second will fail to meet this criterion and therefore, your entire page will not pass.
