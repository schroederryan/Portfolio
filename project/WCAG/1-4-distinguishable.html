1.4 Distinguishable

October 10th, 2016

Definition

“Make it easier for users to see and hear content including separating foreground from background” - World Wide Web Consortium.

What's that mean?

Essentially, you shouldn’t rely on one method to distinguish two areas of your website. These areas could be separating headers from main content, text from media, or even different data on a bar graph.

Why?

It’s important to understand that what might be easily distinguishable to you might not be easily distinguishable to others or those with a disability. Additionally, screen readers cannot pick up styles used throughout a website. This means that using a different color to separate one block of content from another will not be understood by those using a screen reader. The contrast of two colors, as stated by WCAG, should be a ratio of 4:5:1 throughout your website.

Types

The following are additional criterion under the Distinguishable guideline that satisfy up to Level AA. There are additional criterion in order to meet Level AAA. These are all elements that should be considered when developing content for your website. For more information on a particular criterion under the Distinguishable guideline, please refer to section 1.4 Distinguishable.

  • Use of Color
  • Audio Controls
  • Contrast
  • Resize Text
  • Images of Text

Example

It is common for designers and developers to use different colors to separate different areas of their website. However, the contrast of these colors can prevent some users from understanding the content. Color blind users might not be able to visualize that the blocks of content are different from one another and the content might not be interpreted as you, the creator, would like it to be. As mentioned, it’s important to keep a contrast ratio of at least 4:5:1 throughout your website to ensure overall understanding.
