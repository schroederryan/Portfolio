I approach this as a way to communicate more effectively to the client as well as the team members and also ensuring that product that is being created is not only useful but is understood by the client. For example, doing research into how the client will be using the product will allow me, as a technical communicator, to better assess what functions need to be communicated to the client and ensuring nothing gets left out that would make it hard for them to use the product.

Employers Don't Hire Based On Grades

February 28th, 2016

Going through high school, the only thing I was worried about was getting into the college of my dreams - Michigan State University. I made sure to do all of my homework, get the best grades that I could, be involved in as many clubs as I could and really do anything that would ensure my acceptance to MSU. When I entered college, I expected it to be much of the same. Focus on school, get good grades, be involved and you will be rewarded with a great job when you graduate. However, about halfway through my first year, after speaking with career advisors and my own academic advisor, I realized I was approaching college all wrong. While none of them came out and said it (they work for the university, they are supposed to tell you to get good grades), I realized that employers really don’t care about your grades. They don’t care that you got a 2.5 in Bio instead of the 3.5 or 4.0 you were hoping for. All they want to see is that you have the experience to make you qualified for the job you’re applying for.
